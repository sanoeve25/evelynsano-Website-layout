GROWTH AND YIELD PERFORMANCE OF TWO RICE VARIETIES (Oryza sativa) AS INFLUNCED BY ORGANIC FERTILIZER APPLICATION GROWN IN BRGY. PAGBALIKAN, CALBAYOG CITY.

CHAPTER 1

Introduction

NATURE AND IMPORTANCE OF THE STUDY

Rice(Oryza sativa is an annual crop in theFamily Gramineceacharacterized by one flowered spikelet, laterally compressed and two short glumes.

A plant that produces an edible grain, the name is also used for the grain itself. Rice is the most important food crop and the major dietary component for more than a third of the worlds population. It serves as ingredients in manufacturing foods such as puff rice, flakes, and native delicacies. It is also used for animal feeds and pet food, edible oil for cooking and other product. (Department of Agriculture, 1988)

According to the United Natiuons Foods and Agriculture Organization(FAO 1998) ,ricesupplies an average of 889 calories per day per person in China. In contrast, riceprovides an average of 82 calories per day per person in United States. Rice is a nutritious food providing about 90% of calories from carbohydrates and as much as 13% of calories from protein.
Providing adequate supply of rice to the increasing population is one of the major problems in the country. Increasing rice production is a great challenge tofarmer as wellas agriculture institution which are involved in researched and in the generation of appropriate technology over the country. It is not enough that thefarmer knows the art of cultivating the crop but also the proper ways by which production and developing more areas, and by increasing theyield per unit area by following someof the recommended technologies ingrowing this crop.

To meet thechallenge of increasing productivity, farmers should know themost efficient cultural practices incultivating this crop. Successful crop production depends onunderstanding how crops develops and grow, how various factorsaffect plant growth and development and how each factors can be modified and managed, hence this study was conducted.

Objective of theStudy

This study was specifically conducted with the following objectives:

1. to compareand assess theeffect of variety and different kinds of organicfertilizers on the growth and yield of rice;

2.to determine the cost and return analysis of rice as applied with different kinds of organic fertilizers

Time and Place of the Study

This study was conducted fromAugustto January, 2009 inthe rice field located at Barangay Pagbalikan, Calbayog City.

Limitation of the Study

The study covered the performance of twotwo lowland rice varieties such as New42 and PSBRc18 as affected by the application of different kinds ,of organic fertilizers such as Guano and vermin compost.
